A bit about me

Hello! My name is Shraddha.



I was born in India, and moved to the US when I was about 5. I grew up in Ithaca, NY and moved to Potomac, MD for middle school and onwards. Then, I went to Wellesley College. I majored in math and economics and graduated in May 2021.

All through Wellesley, I was very interested in pursuing a career in academia in Economics since it would allow me to study our world analytically and use data to answer questions about peoples’ day-to-day lives. Thus, I worked as a research assistant to various math and economics professors since my first summer of college and got the opportunity to work with extremely large datasets. I was fascinated by the vast amount of data we had at our fingertips and really wanted to use it to effectively to answer interesting questions about the world. But, I was noticing that it was hard to do so without having strong computer science skills to manipulate the data. So, I worked on mastering R and Stata (the two most commonly used programs in economics and statistics).

So after graduation, I still wanted to pursue a career in Economics but was more aware of the fact that I needed to work on my programming skills to be as productive as possible. So while working at Harvard Business School as a research associate, I tried to teach myself various programing languages like SQL and C to be a more effective worker.

At HBS, I was working on a research project which aimed to understand how the #meToo movement differed across sectors using Twitter data. The biggest challenge was to map Twitter users to job sectors using the information the users provide in their bios. Since we had millions of data points, we would have to use programming tools and machine learning models to accomplish this task. So I spent the past year researching models and papers and attempting to implement them, along with other research associate tasks such as literature review and copy editing. During this time, I became even more convinced that to truly harness the power of the immense amount of data we have access to now, I needed to be a much better programmer. Even though I had developed a strong background in R and Stata, I needed formal training to become as effective of a programmer as possible. Thus, I decided to enroll in General Assembly’s Immersive Software Engineering program.

After this program, I really hope to combine my background in statistics and data science from undergrad and my new programming skills to understand the world a little bit better. As of right now, we have access to more data and more types of data than we have ever had before. I really want to keep learning to be a better seeker of answers using data and computer science.
