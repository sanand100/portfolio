A bit about me

Hello! My name is Shraddha.

I was born in India, and moved to the US when I was about 5. I grew up in Ithaca, NY and Potomac, MD. I graduated from Wellesley College in May 2021 with a double major in mathematics and economics.

At Wellesley, I had the unique opportunity to be a cross-registered student at Massachusetts Institute of Technology. Along with taking classes at MIT's economics department, I also participated in MIT's Undergraduate Research Opportunities Program (UROP) and worked under the guidance of various economics professors.

Through working as a UROP research assistant at MIT, as well as other research assistantships at Wellesley, I got the opportunity to work with extremely large datasets. I was fascinated by the vast amount of data we had at our fingertips and wanted to use it to effectively to answer interesting questions about the world. A strong background in computer science was needed to be an effective researcher and manipulate the large datasets. So, I worked on mastering R and Stata (the two most commonly used programs in economics and statistics).

After graduation, I wanted to pursue a career in Economics research but was more aware of the fact that I needed to work on my programming skills to be as productive as possible. So while working at Harvard Business School as a research associate, I taught myself various programing languages like SQL and C to be a more effective worker.

At HBS, I was working on a research project which aimed to understand how the #meToo movement differed across sectors using Twitter data. The biggest challenge was to map Twitter users to job sectors using the information the users provide in their bios. Since we had millions of data points, we knew we had to use programming tools and machine learning models to accomplish this task. I spent the past year researching models and papers and attempting to implement them. During this time, I became even more convinced that to truly harness the power of the immense amount of data we have access to now, I needed to be a much better programmer. Even though I had developed a strong background in R and Stata, I needed formal training to become as effective of a programmer as possible. Thus, I decided to enroll in General Assemblyâ€™s Immersive Software Engineering program.

After this program, I really hope to combine my background in statistics and data science from undergrad and my new programming skills to understand the world a little bit better. As of right now, we have access to more data and more types of data than we have ever had before and I want to take advantage of this to study interesting problems. I want to keep learning to be a better seeker of answers using data and computer science.
